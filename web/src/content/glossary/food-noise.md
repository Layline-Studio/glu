**Food noise** is the constant, intrusive stream of thoughts about food: planning the next meal while still eating this one, negotiating with yourself about the snack drawer, replaying what you ate and what you "should" eat. It's not ordinary hunger — it's the *volume* of food thinking that runs in the background all day.

The term became mainstream around 2023, when people starting GLP-1 medications kept reaching for the same phrase to describe the most surprising effect: not smaller portions, but *silence*. Many describe realizing hours had passed without thinking about food at all — for some, the first time in decades.

## Why it matters

Food noise is a useful concept because it separates two things that diet culture usually blends together: willpower and biology. Someone with loud food noise isn't weaker than someone without it — their brain is generating more frequent, more insistent food cues. GLP-1 receptor agonists act on appetite-regulation pathways in the brain (not just the stomach), which is the leading explanation for why the noise drops so sharply for many people.

## Related terms

Food noise quieting is often the first effect people notice, before weight change — and its *return* toward the end of a dose week is one of the most commonly logged patterns. Tracking when the noise creeps back can be genuinely useful information for a titration conversation with your prescriber.

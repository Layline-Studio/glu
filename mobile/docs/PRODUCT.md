# Product

## What Is Glu

Glu is a wellness companion app for people on medication-assisted weight loss, diabetes management, or metabolic health journeys. It gives users a single, calm, beautifully simple place to log what matters — doses, weight, food, water, movement, and symptoms — and surfaces patterns that help them stay consistent and in control.

The pitch in one sentence: **Glu is the all-in-one wellness companion built for the medication journey — dead simple to use, powered by AI insights, and designed to make progress something you can see, feel, and share.**

---

## The Problem

Taking a weight loss or metabolic health medication is not a passive experience. Users are managing a complex, emotionally charged process that most health apps were never designed for.

### What they're dealing with

- **Overwhelm** — Tracking medication schedules, injection sites, doses, side effects, food, water, weight, and exercise across multiple apps or nothing at all.
- **Side effect anxiety** — Nausea, fatigue, GI issues, and other symptoms appear unexpectedly. Without a log, users can't tell if things are improving, worsening, or tied to specific behaviors.
- **Fear of regain** — Especially for users maintaining or weaning off medication, the fear of losing progress without structure is real and constant.
- **Shame and self-judgment** — Many users carry stigma around needing medication to manage their weight. They don't want a punishing fitness tracker. They want something that meets them where they are.
- **Invisibility of progress** — Weight loss is slow and non-linear. Without a way to see trends over time, users lose motivation during plateaus.
- **Disconnection from their care team** — Most users have no structured way to share their day-to-day journey with their doctor or specialist, leading to appointments where the provider has limited context.
- **Fragmented tools** — There is no single app that combines medication tracking, symptom logging, nutrition, hydration, exercise, and goal management in one place designed specifically for this journey.

### The emotional core

Underneath all of this is a person trying to do something hard — take back control of their health — and feeling like the tools available weren't made for them. They're not training for a marathon. They're not counting macros to compete. They are navigating a medical journey that touches their identity, their body image, their daily routine, and their relationship with food — all at once.

Glu exists to make that journey feel less alone and more manageable.

## Audience

### Primary Persona — The Medication Navigator

- **Age:** 13+
- **Gender:** Any
- **Geography:** Global
- **Medication:** Any weight loss, GLP-1, diabetes, or metabolic health medication (injectable or pill) — including but not limited to Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda, Victoza, Trulicity, Tirzepatide, Semaglutide, Retatrutide, Rybelsus
- **Health goals:** Lose weight, maintain weight, manage diabetes, manage PCOS, improve heart health

### What they look like in practice

| Segment | Profile |
|---|---|
| **The New Starter** | Just started or about to start medication. Excited but nervous. Wants structure from day one. |
| **The Adjuster** | A few months in, navigating dose changes and side effects. Needs a clear log to understand their body. |
| **The Maintainer** | Has hit their goal and is focused on not regaining. Needs accountability and pattern visibility. |
| **The Weaner** | Coming off medication. Wants to preserve habits and catch early warning signs of regain. |
| **The Chronic Manager** | Using medication long-term for diabetes or PCOS. Needs consistent, low-friction daily tracking. |

### What they all share

- They feel underserved by existing wellness apps that weren't designed for their context
- They want simplicity above all — they're already managing a lot
- They are health-conscious but not fitness-obsessed
- They value calm, non-judgmental support
- They want to be able to show their doctor what's been happening

---

## The Solution

Glu is built around three principles:

1. **One place for everything** — Medication, body metrics, food, water, movement, symptoms. No switching apps.
2. **Dead simple to use** — Logging a dose, a weight check-in, or a glass of water takes seconds. No friction, no unnecessary fields.
3. **Progress you can see and share** — Trends, patterns, and milestones are surfaced clearly, and made shareable — for motivation, for social content, or for a doctor's appointment.

## Current Features

### Daily Experience

#### Home Overview
**Problem it solves:** Users need one place that answers "how am I doing today?" without making them dig through separate logs.

**What it does:**
- Surfaces today's progress across water, meals, calories, protein, fiber, exercise, mood, symptoms, weight, and dose adherence
- Adapts cards and progress states based on the user's active goals
- Uses quick entry points into the main logging flows from the home screen

**Benefit:** Users can open Glu and understand their day at a glance, then act immediately.

---

#### Suggested Actions
**Problem it solves:** Some of the highest-value features are not part of a rigid daily habit, so users need them surfaced clearly instead of buried in navigation.

**What it does:**
- Highlights a small set of helpful actions on the home screen under **Suggested actions**
- Currently surfaces:
  - **Portion Check** — a camera-based portion recommendation flow
  - **Glow Up** — a before/after progress image flow

**Benefit:** Users get to the right action faster, especially for one-off moments like a meal decision or a progress photo update.

---

### Free Tier

#### Dose Tracking
**Problem it solves:** Users forget whether they've taken their dose, can't track injection site rotation, and lose the history needed to spot adherence patterns.

**What it does:**
- Log injections or pills with date, medication, and dose
- Track injection site rotation across 14 body locations (abdomen, thighs, arms, buttocks) with automatic next-site suggestions
- Maintain a full dose history with notes

**Benefit:** Never wonder if you took your dose. Know exactly where you injected last week.

---

#### Symptom Logging
**Problem it solves:** Side effects are unpredictable and hard to describe at a doctor's appointment without a record. Users also can't tell if symptoms are getting better or tied to specific behaviors.

**What it does:**
- Log overall feeling (great / good / okay / bad)
- Select from 19 tracked symptoms (nausea, fatigue, constipation, food noise, hair loss, etc.)
- Rate severity (mild / moderate / severe)
- Add optional notes
- Show a consistent note indicator in logged entries when a note is present across note-enabled log types

**Benefit:** Walk into any appointment with a real log. Spot whether nausea peaks after certain meals, or whether symptoms are improving on a new dose.

---

#### Weight Tracking
**Problem it solves:** Weight is non-linear and users lose motivation during plateaus. Without trend data, a single bad weigh-in feels like failure.

**What it does:**
- Log weight in kg or lb
- Set a goal weight with BMI context
- View weight trend over 7 days, 30 days, 3 months, or 1 year
- See moving averages to smooth noise

**Benefit:** See the actual trend beneath the daily fluctuations. Understand how far you've come even when today's number feels discouraging.

---

#### Hydration Tracking
**Problem it solves:** GLP-1 users are at higher risk of dehydration due to reduced appetite and food-sourced water. Many don't drink enough.

**What it does:**
- Log water intake with one tap using quick-add presets
- Set a daily or weekly water goal
- Track progress vs. goal across the selected time range

**Benefit:** Stay on top of hydration without thinking about it. Know if you're consistently falling short.

---

#### Nutrition Logging
**Problem it solves:** Users want to track eating patterns without the obsessive calorie-counting culture of traditional diet apps.

**What it does:**
- Log meals with calories and full macros (carbs, protein, fat, fiber, sugar)
- Support manual entry plus optional note and photo attachment
- Support "consumed" tracking so users can record all or part of a meal
- Set a daily or weekly goal by meal count or calorie target
- Track trends over time

**Benefit:** Know whether you're eating enough (under-eating is a real risk on GLP-1s). Track what you want to track without judgment.

---

#### Exercise Tracking
**Problem it solves:** Movement is often the first thing dropped when side effects hit. Users need a frictionless way to maintain the habit.

**What it does:**
- Log activity type, duration, and intensity (light / moderate / intense)
- Set daily or weekly movement goals
- View daily and weekly activity trends

**Benefit:** See the cumulative picture of your movement. Celebrate consistency even when individual sessions feel small.

---

#### Glow Up
**Problem it solves:** Scale weight alone is emotionally thin. Users need a visual way to see progress during slow, non-linear phases.

**What it does:**
- Lets users create a side-by-side before/after image from two photos
- Saves the final comparison to the user's gallery
- Persists the before photo, after photo, and final composed image to storage under one daily record
- Keeps one Glow Up entry per day, replacing duplicates for the same date

**Benefit:** Progress becomes visible in a way that is easier to feel and share than a chart alone.

---

#### Progress Charts
**Problem it solves:** Users can't see their own trajectory without visualizations. Progress is invisible without the right view.

**What it does:**
- Overview dashboard for all tracked metrics
- Individual deep-dive screens for each category
- Time range selector (7D / 30D / 3M / 1Y) that adapts to available data
- Bar charts, line charts, and severity charts per category
- Goal reference lines overlaid on charts

**Benefit:** See the full story of your health journey. Understand what's working, what needs attention, and how far you've come.

---

#### Goal Setting
**Problem it solves:** Most users know roughly what they want but have no structured way to commit to targets and track progress against them.

**What it does:**
- Set independent goals for water, exercise, meals, and weight
- Choose daily or weekly timeframes
- Support calorie-based meal goals used by Portion Check
- Toggle goals on and off as priorities shift
- Goals maintain a history of changes over time

**Benefit:** Set realistic targets that match your life right now. Adjust as you evolve. Always know where you stand.

---

#### Onboarding Personalization
**Problem it solves:** Generic wellness apps feel irrelevant fast. Users on this journey have very specific contexts that require tailored guidance.

**What it does:**
- 20-step adaptive onboarding that captures medication details, body metrics, health goals, daily routine, and symptom priorities
- Personalizes all app copy, guidance, and goal defaults to the user's specific situation
- Supports any medication (injectable or pill), any health goal, any measurement unit (metric or imperial)

**Benefit:** From the first session, Glu feels like it was made for you specifically — not a generic health tracker with your name on it.

---

### Pro Tier

#### AI Meal Capture
**Problem it solves:** Manual nutrition logging is the biggest source of friction in food tracking, especially when appetite is low and users want the fastest possible path to "done."

**What it does:**
- Supports camera, text, and audio meal capture in the meal log
- Uses AI to estimate calories, carbs, protein, fat, fiber, and sugar
- Pre-fills the meal form for fast confirmation and editing

**Benefit:** Logging a meal becomes much closer to describing it than manually building it from scratch.

**Why it's Pro:** This is a clear time-saver that materially reduces logging friction, making it a strong premium upgrade.

---

#### Smart Reminders — Dose
**Problem it solves:** Missing a dose is the most common failure point on a medication journey. Without a scheduled prompt, users rely on memory alone.

**What it does:**
- Set a next-dose reminder with a custom schedule (daily, weekly, every 14 days, or custom intervals)
- Reminder is visible from the home screen dose card
- Can be rescheduled or dismissed at any time

**Benefit:** Never miss a scheduled dose again. The reminder adapts to any medication frequency.

**Why it's Pro:** Reminders are one of the highest-value features for adherence. Keeping them behind Pro creates a clear, tangible reason to upgrade from day one — especially for new users still building their medication routine.

---

#### Portion Check — AI Portion Advisor
**Problem it solves:** Users often understand what they should eat in theory, but still need help deciding how much of a real meal fits their day.

**What it does:**
- Starts from the home screen Suggested actions area
- Uses the camera to analyze a meal and estimate macros and calories
- Combines that meal estimate with the user's current daily calorie target and calories already consumed
- Recommends what portion of the meal fits the remaining budget
- Can hand the result straight into meal logging with a prefilled consumed amount

**Benefit:** Bridges the gap between planning and eating. It gives users a practical "how much of this plate?" answer at the moment of decision.

**Why it's Pro:** It extends AI meal capture into real-time guidance, which is more valuable than logging alone and depends on a calorie-based meal goal.

## Planned Features

### Pro Tier

#### Supplements Tracking
**Problem it solves:** Many users on GLP-1s are advised to take supplements to counteract nutrient depletion (iron, B12, magnesium, vitamin D, etc.) but have no way to manage them alongside their medication routine.

**What it does:**
- Log daily supplement intake (type, dose, timing)
- Set individual reminders per supplement
- Maintain a supplement history alongside medication logs

**Benefit:** Never forget your supplements. Keep your full daily health routine in one place — medication, supplements, and lifestyle — without switching apps.

**Why it's Pro:** Supplements sit alongside the medication routine and reminder system, both of which are Pro. Keeping them together makes the Pro tier more coherent and compelling.

---

#### AI Health Insights
**Problem it solves:** Users log consistently but can't spot the patterns hidden in their own data.

**What it does:**
- Surface correlations the user can't see themselves. Examples:
  - "Your nausea logs cluster within 6 hours of eating high-fat meals."
  - "Your average weight drops 0.4 kg in weeks where you exercise 3+ times."
  - "You tend to miss doses on weekends — want to adjust your reminder?"

**Benefit:** Converts logging into learning. Makes the data users are already collecting genuinely actionable.

**Why it's Pro:** This is the intelligence layer that makes all the data logged in the free tier genuinely valuable.

---

#### Body Scan — AI Body Composition Estimator
**Problem it solves:** Weight alone is a poor proxy for body composition changes, especially on GLP-1s where muscle mass preservation matters.

**What it does:**
- Snap a photo and Glu estimates body fat percentage range using AI
- Tracks changes over time alongside weight for a more complete picture of body composition

**Benefit:** A richer health signal without expensive equipment.

**Why it's Pro:** Weight alone is a poor proxy for body composition changes — especially on GLP-1s, where muscle mass preservation matters. This gives users a richer signal without expensive equipment.

---

#### Glow Up — Before & After Progress Visuals
**Problem it solves:** Months of quiet consistency are invisible without a way to make them tangible.

**What it does:**
- Generate before-and-after images or videos of the user's physical journey using logged data, photos, and milestones
- Shareable to social media or kept privately

**Benefit:** Turns months of quiet consistency into a visible, celebratable moment.

**Why it's Pro:** The most emotionally powerful feature in the app. Also the highest-potential organic marketing asset — user-generated Glow Up content is shareable, relatable, and drives installs naturally.

---

#### Doctor Report Export
**Problem it solves:** Doctors currently have no structured visibility into the day-to-day experience of their GLP-1 patients.

**What it does:**
- Generate a structured PDF summary of health data for a given time period — doses, weight trend, symptoms, side effects, exercise
- Ready to bring to an appointment or share with a specialist

**Benefit:** Walk into any appointment with a real record. Give your doctor actual context instead of trying to remember weeks of data on the spot.

**Why it's Pro:** Solves a real unmet need. Addresses the disconnect between patients and care teams that generic apps don't serve.

---

#### Extended History & Data Export
**Problem it solves:** Power users and people tracking long-term journeys need more than a rolling window of history.

**What it does:**
- Unlimited data history (free tier has a rolling window)
- CSV or PDF export of all raw data

**Benefit:** Your full journey, always accessible and portable.

**Why it's Pro:** High value for serious long-term users without adding cost to the free tier.

---

#### Advanced Analytics
**Problem it solves:** The overview charts are useful but don't answer deeper questions about what's driving progress.

**What it does:**
- Correlation views (e.g., sleep vs. symptoms, exercise vs. weight)
- Custom date range analysis
- Streak tracking and consistency scores

**Benefit:** Goes beyond what's useful for casual trackers into territory that serious users find genuinely motivating.

**Why it's Pro:** Goes beyond what's useful for casual trackers into territory that serious users find genuinely motivating.

## Competitive Positioning

### Who exists in this space

| Product | What it is | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| MyFitnessPal | Calorie / macro tracker | No medication tracking, no symptoms, no GLP-1 context, cluttered |
| Noom | Behavioral weight loss coaching | Subscription coaching model, no medication tracking, opinionated diet approach |

| Generic GLP-1 apps | Dose reminders only | Single-purpose, no lifestyle context |

### Glu's position

**Glu is the only all-in-one wellness companion built specifically for the medication journey — simple enough to use every day, smart enough to surface what matters, and designed to turn personal health data into something shareable.**

The three core differentiators:
1. **Purpose-built for this context** — Every feature, every piece of copy, every default was designed around the GLP-1 / metabolic medication journey. Not retrofitted from a generic fitness app.
2. **Radical simplicity** — Logging any metric takes seconds. The app never adds friction in the name of comprehensiveness.
3. **Progress made visible and shareable** — AI camera features, Glow Up visuals, and doctor reports make data useful beyond the act of logging.

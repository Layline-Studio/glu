## Positioning

### What We Are Selling Now

Glu should no longer be positioned in screenshots as only an all-in-one tracker.

The stronger story now is:

**Glu is a medication-focused daily companion that helps users stay on track, make better decisions in the moment, and see progress beyond the scale.**

That means the screenshot set should emphasize:

1. **Daily guidance** — a clear daily tracker users can check fast
2. **Action-oriented AI** — Portion Check and Snap Macro
3. **Visible progress** — Glow Up and trends
4. **Supportive routines** — reminders and flexible goals

### Primary Value Proposition

**Track your medication journey and get daily guidance that helps you stay on track.**

### Supporting Message

**Track your routine, use AI at mealtime, stay on top of reminders, and see progress you can actually feel.**

## Screenshots

### 1 — Hero / Home

**Visual:** Home screen showing only the tracker area. It should feel calm, complete, and immediately medication-relevant without relying on the suggested actions area.

**Headline:** Your GLP-1 journey,\nall in one place.

**Description:** Track doses, symptoms, meals, water, movement, and progress in one calm place.

**Why it matters:** This establishes the category fast. Users should immediately understand this is not a generic calorie app or fitness tracker.

---

### 2 — Portion Check

**Visual:** Portion Check result screen after camera analysis, showing the meal image, recommended portion ring, and daily calorie context. The portion percentage should be visually obvious.

**Headline:** Know how much\nto eat at every meal.

**Description:** Point at your meal and Glu estimates the portion that fits your daily calorie goal.

**Why it matters:** This is one of the strongest acquisition features in the app. It is practical, immediate, and easy to understand at a glance.

---

### 3 — Glow Up

**Why it matters:** This is the most emotional feature in the product. It makes the app feel motivating, not just functional.

### 4 — Snap Macro

**Why it matters:** This supports the AI story while reinforcing ease of use. It tells users the app reduces logging friction, not adds to it.

## What To Remove From The Old Story

The previous screenshot narrative leaned too heavily on:

- "all in one place"
- generic tracking
- rigid medication setup
- one-size-fits-all goal framing

Those still matter, but they are no longer the sharpest top-of-funnel story.

For acquisition, **Portion Check** and **Glow Up** are stronger than a pure medication-setup story.

If a screenshot must be cut, cut reminders before either of those.

---

## Visual Direction

- Keep the backgrounds bright, calm, and premium
- Avoid crowded composite screens; each screenshot should communicate one idea fast
- Make sure device content is readable without needing the subtitle
- Prefer real app states over conceptual mockups
- If using Glow Up, choose a transformation that is clearly visible at App Store thumbnail sizes
- If using Portion Check, make the portion recommendation the focal point, not the supporting stats
- For Reminders, show both dose and supplement support clearly in one screen
- For Progress, prefer the trend chart over a generic stats summary
- For Goals, make the flexibility of the targets obvious in the UI

---

## Final Story We Want The Listing To Tell

**Glu helps people on GLP-1 medications stay organized, make better decisions at every meal, and actually see their progress.**

That is the App Store message now.

It is more specific and more compelling than "track everything in one place."

---

## Secondary Features

Keep **Symptoms** as a secondary feature that can be referenced in supporting copy or later variants, but it does not need to be in the main 7-screen sequence right now.
